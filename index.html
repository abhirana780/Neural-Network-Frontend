🎮 Level 1

Meet the AI Brain

Artificial Neural Networks are inspired by the human brain. Just like neurons in your brain pass signals to each other, an ANN passes information through layers of digital neurons to make decisions.

📖 What is a Neural Network?

Imagine you're a sales manager trying to predict whether a new lead will convert into a customer. You look at several things — their age, income, and how many times they visited your website. Then you weigh up all those factors and make a decision.

A Neural Network does exactly the same thing — automatically, and at scale. It takes raw data (inputs), processes it through layers of "thinking" steps (hidden layers), and produces a final decision (output).

📥
Input Layer
Receives raw data — like a form filled by the customer: age, income, website visits.
🔮
Hidden Layer
The "thinking" stage. Each neuron weighs the inputs and decides how strongly it should respond.
📤
Output Layer
Gives the final answer — e.g. 78% chance this customer will buy.
Input Layer Hidden Layer Output Layer
🏢 Real Business Analogy
Think of this as a smart credit officer at a bank. The officer receives your application (Input), evaluates your history using judgment and experience (Hidden Layer), and gives you a loan decision (Output). The Neural Network learns to do this automatically — and improves with every application it sees.
💡
Key takeaway: The lines connecting the neurons are called weights. They determine how much each input matters. Learning = adjusting these weights until the AI makes good predictions.

⚡ Level 3

Forward Propagation Game

Watch your AI brain process a customer's information in real-time. Signals flow from left (Input) → through the middle (Hidden) → to the answer (Output).

⚡ What is Forward Propagation?

Forward propagation is the AI making a prediction. It's the equivalent of a manager reviewing a customer file and reaching a conclusion. Here's exactly what happens when you click the button:

1

Customer data enters — Age, Income & Web Visits are scaled to values between 0 and 1 (normalization) so the AI can process them equally.

2

Hidden layer activates — Each hidden neuron multiplies each input by its "weight" (importance), adds them up, and applies the sigmoid function to produce a value 0–1.

3

Output produced — The final neuron combines the hidden layer outputs into one number: the purchase probability %. Above 50% = predicted buyer.

📈 Level 4

Activation Functions

Every neuron has an activation function — a gatekeeper that decides whether the neuron should "fire" or stay quiet. We use the Sigmoid function, which squishes any value between 0 and 1.

🤔 Why does a neuron need an activation function?

Without an activation function, the network would just be doing simple addition and multiplication — it couldn't learn complex patterns. The activation function introduces non-linearity, which means the AI can learn nuanced, curved relationships in data.

Think of it like this: a simple rule says "income > \$50k → buy." But reality is more complex — it depends on age + income + visits all together. The activation function lets the AI model these complex combinations.

🔄 Level 5

Backpropagation – AI Learns from Mistakes

When the AI makes a wrong prediction, it doesn't give up — it learns! The error signal travels backward through the network, tweaking every connection weight to reduce future errors.

🔄 What is Backpropagation?

Backpropagation = "Learning from your mistakes, automatically." Here's exactly what happens when you click the button:

1

AI makes a prediction (Forward Pass) — First the network predicts whether this customer will buy, using the current weights.

2

Error is measured — We compare the prediction to the actual answer. A big gap = big error. A small gap = small error.

3

Error flows backward — The error signal travels back through the network. Each connection's weight is adjusted slightly to reduce the error. This is called Gradient Descent.

4

New prediction is better — After the weight update, the network makes a slightly more accurate prediction. Repeat this thousands of times = a trained AI.

🏆 Level 6

Train Your AI – Watch It Improve!

This is where everything comes together. Press Start Training and watch your AI go from random guesses to accurate predictions — all by itself.

🚀 What does "Training" mean?

Training is the AI repeating the learning cycle (forward prop + backprop) hundreds of times across all customer examples. With each pass through the data, it fine-tunes its weights — getting slightly better each time.

1

Epoch = one complete pass through all 10 customers. After each epoch, weights are updated and the AI is a little smarter.

2

Accuracy = what % of the 10 customers the AI correctly classified ("will buy" or "won't buy"). 100% accuracy means the AI correctly identifies every customer.

3

The chart shows how accuracy improves over time. A rising curve = the AI is learning. A flat curve = the AI has hit its limit (may need more data or neurons).

💡 Tip: 50 epochs is usually enough for this small dataset. Try 100 for even higher accuracy. More epochs = more learning cycles.
